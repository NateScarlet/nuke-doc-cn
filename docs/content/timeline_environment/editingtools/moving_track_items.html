Moving Clip Instances

Click and drag the selected clip instance(s) to the required position on the timeline. A time count popup, in conjunction with the snap to edit function, helps you to reposition the clip instance(s) accurately.

You can also move clip instances up and down the track hierarchy using drag-and-drop or the Alt+, (comma) and Alt+. (period) keyboard shortcuts.

The following table describes the Move/Trim tool’s modifiers and actions:

Overwrite

The default move mode. The dragged clip instance overwrites any items that are present in the move location.

Ripple

drag then hold Alt and drop

Drag-and-drop clip instances on top of other items without overwriting content - items are pushed down the timeline to accommodate the move.

Duplicate

hold Alt and drag then release Alt and drop

 

Copy the clip instance, then drag-and-drop on top of other items overwriting existing content - items are not pushed down the timeline to accommodate the move.

Ripple and Duplicate

Copy the clip instance, then drag-and-drop items on top of others without overwriting content - items are pushed down the timeline to accommodate the move.

NOTE:  On Linux, hold Ctrl+Alt for Duplicate and Ripple Duplicate modifiers.

Remove the selected clip instance(s) and ripple items down stream to close gaps in the timeline.

NOTE:  The ripple effect may not close gaps entirely, because Nuke Studio does not allow linked tracks to become desynchronized during rippling.

If you need to nudge clip instances horizontally by just a frame or two, you can select the items on the timeline and press , (comma) to nudge it left or . (period) to nudge it right. Press Shift+, (comma) or . (period) to nudge the clip instance horizontally by the Frame Increment set under the Viewer.

NOTE:  You cannot overwrite other clip instances on the timeline horizontally using the nudge keys. However, vertical nudging (Alt+, and Alt+.) overwrites other tracks.
